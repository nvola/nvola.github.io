ABOUT

PROFILE

Natasha VÁzquez is a graduate student and a public health researcher. She is a life-long student and a quick learner with interests that span various fields. She is passionate about the intersection between information technologies and public health. In alignment with her studies and career goals, she is interested in data analytics, data engineering, database administration, and other related fields.

EDUCATION & EMPLOYMENT

Natasha VÁzquez began her undergraduate career at New York University studying Applied Psychology, then transferred to the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. In May 2015, she graduated With Highest Distinction with a Bachelor's of Science in Psychology, with a second major in Hispanic Linguistics. As a result of her academic achievement as an undergraduate, she was invited to join Phi Beta Kappa honor society, and became a member in 2015.

In addition to working part-time during her undergraduate studies, she also worked as an English teacher for a summer in Budapest, Hungary and served in two psychology research laboratories. In these labs, she contributed to principal investigator- and graduate student-led projects. Further, she led a Spanish-version study of a project conducted by a graduate student in the lab under the guidance of the principal investigator.

After graduating, Natasha accepted her current position at RTI International as a public health analyst in the Social Innovation & Communication Strategy program within the Center for Communication Science. She contributes to various public health research projects.

As of January 2017, Natasha is a Master's Student in Information Science at UNC's School of Information and Library Science. She is specializing in Database Design & Development and Data Analytics. Her expected date of graduation is May 2019.
